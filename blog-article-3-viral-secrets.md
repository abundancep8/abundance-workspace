## The Algorithm Signals (What Platforms Actually Reward)

### YouTube Shorts Algorithm

**Highest priority:**
1. **Watch time (completion rate)** - Do people finish the video?
2. **Shares** - Do people send to friends?
3. **Saves** - Is it reference-worthy?
4. **Comments** - Did it spark conversation?
5. **Rewatches** - Do people watch again?

**Strategy:** Create content people finish, share, and rewatch.

Hook in 1 second. Pacing keeps changing (edit every 2-3 seconds max).

End with cliffhanger or emotional payoff = rewatch.

### TikTok Algorithm

**Highest priority:**
1. **Watch time** (how long you watch)
2. **Engagement velocity** (immediate vs slow engagement)
3. **Completion rate** (watch entire video?)
4. **Repeat watches** (watch multiple times?)
5. **Shares** (sent to friends?)

**Strategy:** TikTok prioritizes *fast* engagement. If it doesn't engage in first 3 seconds, it gets buried.

Hook must hit HARD.

Pacing = fast cuts, trending audio, visual interest.

First 3 seconds = make or break.

### X/Twitter Algorithm

**Highest priority:**
1. **Engagement velocity** (replies, retweets, likes within first hour)
2. **Network effects** (who's engaging - influencers > regular users)
3. **Quote tweets** (highest value engagement)
4. **Click-through** (people clicking links)
5. **Impressions** (reach)

**Strategy:** X rewards threads (5+ tweets get 3-5x more engagement than single tweets).

First tweet must create curiosity.

Each subsequent tweet builds value.

Last tweet has CTA + link.

## The Content That Converts (Not Just Entertains)

Most creators optimize for views.

Smart creators optimize for *conversions*.

**Views ≠ Money**
**Conversions = Money**

**The conversion-optimized video structure:**

1. **Hook (2 sec):** Stop the scroll with pattern interrupt
2. **Problem (3-5 sec):** Name a pain point they feel
3. **Insight (8-12 sec):** Share a surprising insight or proof
4. **Solution (5 sec):** Show how to solve it (or where to learn)
5. **CTA (2-3 sec):** "Link in bio" or "Subscribe for more"

**Example video breakdown:**

Hook: "I made $4,200 in 30 days with zero experience"
Problem: "Most people think you need years of setup"
Insight: "Here's what actually matters [3 specific insights]"
Solution: "Use this system [link to course]"
CTA: "Link in bio if you're serious"

**Length:** 60 seconds max

**Completion rate:** Should be 70%+ (YouTube/TikTok measure this)

## The Metrics That Actually Matter

**Vanity metrics (don't matter for growth):**
- Total views
- Total followers
- Total likes

**Real metrics (these drive algorithm boost):**
- Watch time percentage (completion rate)
- Engagement rate (engagements / views)
- Share rate (shares / views)
- Save rate (saves / views)
- Click-through rate (clicks / views)
- Repeat watch rate (rewatches / views)

**Pro tip:** A video with 1,000 views and 15% engagement > 10,000 views and 1% engagement.

The algorithm sees engagement rate.

## The Psychological Principles Behind Viral Content

### 1. Cognitive Dissonance
Your brain loves resolving contradictions.

"AI will replace you" vs. "AI will multiply you"
= Brain engages (wants resolution)

### 2. Curiosity Gap
Open loops in your brain create tension.

"I discovered something about the algorithm" (but don't say what)
= Brain watches whole video (wants closure)

### 3. Social Proof
Humans follow the herd.

"500 people signed up" = Brain thinks "must be valuable"

### 4. Scarcity
Limited availability = higher perceived value.

"Only 1% know this" = Brain thinks "rare information"

### 5. Reciprocity
When you give value, people feel obligated.

Share 3 free insights = People feel they owe you = Higher conversion

## What I Learned About What Works

**Works:** Specific numbers (not vague claims)
**Doesn't work:** Generic advice ("work hard and believe")

**Works:** Personal story (I did this, here's proof)
**Doesn't work:** Theoretical advice (here's how others did it)

**Works:** Showing struggle + solution (vulnerability)
**Doesn't work:** Pretending it was easy (inauthentic)

**Works:** Fast pacing + constant cuts (engagement)
**Doesn't work:** Long monologues (people exit)

**Works:** Curiosity hook + payoff (satisfying ending)
**Doesn't work:** Vague hooks + weak payoff (disappointed viewers)
