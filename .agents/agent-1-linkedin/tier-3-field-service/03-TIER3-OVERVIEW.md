## Tier 3: Unified Pain Points

### Pain Point #1: Dispatching & Route Optimization
**Severity:** CRITICAL (time = money)
- Field teams don't know where to go until call comes in
- Route inefficiency (wasted drive time)
- No coordination between jobs (could combine calls, save time)
- Customer wait time increases (no ETA communication)
- Fuel costs skyrocket (inefficient routes)

**Impact:** 15-30% of revenue wasted on inefficient travel
**Our Message:** "Smart routing saves 10+ hours/week of drive time"

**Specific to Field Service:**
- GPS tracking (where are my teams right now?)
- Optimal route sequencing (shortest total drive time)
- Job clustering (combine nearby calls)
- Real-time updates to customer (I'll arrive at 2:15 PM)

---

### Pain Point #2: Job Scheduling & Capacity
**Severity:** CRITICAL
- Manual scheduling (calls, texts, emails)
- Double-bookings happen (one person overbooked)
- No visibility into technician availability
- Emergency calls disrupt entire day
- Can't tell customer realistic appointment time

**Impact:** Overbooking or underselling capacity; customer frustration
**Our Message:** "See real-time capacity. Promise realistic dates. Overbook never."

---

### Pain Point #3: Job Estimating & Quoting
**Severity:** HIGH
- On-site estimating time-consuming
- No system for quoting (manual, inconsistent)
- Estimate accuracy poor (scope creep)
- No communication between estimate and work order
- Customers don't know what they'll pay until after work

**Impact:** Time wasted, revenue lost to scope creep, customer shock at invoices
**Our Message:** "AI-powered estimating in the field. Customer approves before work starts."

---

### Pain Point #4: Invoicing & Payment Delays
**Severity:** HIGH (cash flow killer)
- Invoice written after job (days later)
- Payment not collected until invoice sent (weeks)
- Some customers don't pay (no follow-up)
- Aged receivables (30-60+ days typical)
- No way to collect payment in field

**Impact:** Cash flow strained; 10-20% receivables never collected
**Our Message:** "Collect 90% of payment before technician leaves job site"

---

### Pain Point #5: Team Communication & Coordination
**Severity:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- Field teams isolated (scattered across geography)
- Admin staff can't reach technicians (when done with job?)
- No way to reassign jobs on the fly
- Parts ordering delayed (tech doesn't know where to get part)
- Before/after photo documentation missing

**Impact:** Efficiency loss, coordination failures, repeat visits
**Our Message:** "Real-time team communication from office to field"

---

### Pain Point #6: Customer Data & History
**Severity:** MEDIUM
- No central record of what was fixed at customer property
- Repeat calls for same issue (should be warranty)
- Service history scattered (paper, memory)
- Warranty management manual
- Preventative maintenance not tracked

**Impact:** Customer service failures, warranty disputes
**Our Message:** "Complete service history + predictive maintenance"

---

### Pain Point #7: Business Visibility
**Severity:** MEDIUM
- Owner doesn't know daily revenue (paid later)
- No idea which jobs are profitable
- Can't track technician performance
- Fleet utilization invisible
- Pricing accuracy unknown

**Impact:** Blind decision-making, pricing errors, efficiency blind spots
**Our Message:** "Profit & loss visible in real-time dashboard"

## Tier 3: Competitive Landscape

### Incumbents (Entrenched but Vulnerable)

**Housecall Pro**
- Strengths: Specific to service trades, mobile app, job management
- Weaknesses: Expensive ($150-400/month), some integration gaps, UI dated
- Price: $150-$400/month depending on features
- Threat: HIGH (direct competitor, but overprice for small)

- Strengths: Enterprise-grade, dispatching, CRM, integration
- Weaknesses: $300-1000+/month, requires implementation, heavy

- Strengths: Purpose-built for field service, mobile-first, good UX
- Weaknesses: $200-500/month, missing some features

**RealBlue**

## Tier 3: Sales Motion

**Longer, consultative sales cycle (30-90 days typical)**

**Week 1-2: Outreach & Discovery**
- Identify pain (scheduling chaos, cash flow, visibility)
- Intro call: "Can I ask about your biggest operational bottleneck?"
- Book working session: "Let's map out your current process"

**Week 2-3: Working Session & Diagnosis**
- 1-hour call: Walk through scheduling, dispatching, invoicing
- Record the pain: "You're losing X to this"
- Show how we'd improve it: "With our system, you'd..."

**Week 3-4: Demo & Trial Setup**
- Live demo showing their data/scenarios
- Free trial: Load 2 weeks of jobs, invite 2-3 technicians to test
- Timeline: "Use it for 10 days. See the difference firsthand."

**Week 4-6: Trial Period**
- Check-in at day 5: "How's the first week? Questions?"
- Highlight wins: "You dispatched 3 emergency calls in 15 minutes. Normally takes 45 min."
- Address concerns: "Worried about adoption? Team says it's intuitive."

**Week 6-8: Close**
- "Ready to scale? Here's onboarding + training plan"
- Go-live: 3-5 business days full implementation
- Ongoing: Success manager for first 90 days

**Key metric:** 45%+ of qualified prospects close in 60 days (consultative sales works here)

## Tier 3: Retention & Expansion

**Sticky factors:**
1. **Data lock-in** — Years of job history, service records, customer data
2. **Team adoption** — Technicians trained; switching means retraining
3. **Integration depth** — Connected to payment processor, customer portal, possibly suppliers
4. **Daily necessity** — Used every single day for dispatching

**Monthly churn target:** <3% (field service is sticky; lower churn than Tier 2)
**Expansion rate:** 20-25% of customers add paid add-ons (analytics, integrations, white-label)

**Upsell Paths:**
1. **Per-technician expansion** — Growing crew → add more licenses
2. **Add-on services** — Advanced analytics, custom integrations
3. **Multi-location rollout** — Success at one location → scale to others
4. **White-label** — Large customers want their brand → $500-1000/month add-on
